The Scan Kiosk

"If you touched it, scan it. No exceptions. No excuses."

This is the heartbeat of the system. The Scan Kiosk is where every part movement gets recorded — OUT when you pull from stock, IN when you return unused parts, ADJUST for corrections, and RECEIVE for new deliveries.

Think of it like this: if the dashboard is the scoreboard, the Kiosk is how you put points on the board. No scans = no data = we're flying blind.

Step 1: Select Your Name. Always. The system tracks who did what. If your name isn't on it, it didn't happen — at least not in a way that helps anyone.

Step 2: Pick Your Mode. OUT is the default (most common). Switch modes before scanning — don't batch OUT and IN parts together.

Step 3: Scan or Search. Use the camera to scan a barcode, or type the part number manually. The system does fuzzy matching — if you type "6800" it'll find part numbers containing that string.

Step 4: Adjust Quantity. Default is 1. Use the +/− buttons or type a number. Pulling 3 of the same part? Set qty to 3 — don't scan it three times.

Step 5: Commit. Hit the green Commit Batch button. Quantities update instantly across all users' screens.

Analyzer Serial — When It's Required

The Analyzer Serial # field ties every part you scan to a specific instrument. This is what powers the Analyzer Analysis page — without it, parts vanish into a black hole.

ModeSerial Required?Why
OUTAlways requiredEvery part pulled must be traced to an analyzer. The system will block you from committing without one.
INDependsThe system asks: "Is this a new part?" → Yes (new stock): Serial not required. → No (correction/return): Serial required.
ADJUSTOptionalCorrections to quantity — not typically tied to a specific analyzer.
RECEIVEOptionalNew stock arriving — not yet assigned to an analyzer.
⚠️ OUT Without a Serial = Blocked

If you try to commit an OUT batch without entering an Analyzer Serial, the system will stop you with a red error. This is intentional. Every part pulled needs a home. No serial, no commit.

💡 The IN Mode Prompt

When you commit an IN batch without a serial, a popup will ask: "Is this a new part?" If it's brand-new stock, tap Yes and proceed. If you're returning a part that was previously pulled from an analyzer, tap No — you'll need to enter that analyzer's serial number first.

Hands-On Exercise 3.1 — Your First Scan (OUT)
  1. Navigate to 📷 Scan Kiosk in the sidebar.
  2. Select your name from the employee dropdown.
  3. Ensure mode is set to OUT (red).
  4. Enter the Analyzer Serial # — this is required for OUT. Notice the red border reminding you.
  5. In the search bar, type a part number you recognize — or scan a barcode from a part nearby.
  6. Verify the part description matches what you're holding.
  7. Set quantity to 1.
  8. Hit Commit Batch.

✅ Verify: Open Transaction Search in a new tab. Filter by your name. See your scan? That's real-time.

✅ Bonus: Try committing an OUT batch without a serial number. The system should block you. That's by design.

Kit Consumption — The Power Move

Kits are pre-built groups of parts that go together (think: a full reagent set for a VITROS run). Instead of scanning 8 individual parts, you select a kit and the system pulls all components at once.

Hands-On Exercise 3.2 — Consume a Kit
  1. In the Scan Kiosk, look for the Kit selector.
  2. Select a kit from the dropdown.
  3. The batch auto-populates with all kit components and quantities.
  4. Enter the Analyzer Serial # — required for OUT mode, even for kits.
  5. Review the batch list — every component should be there.
  6. Hit Commit Batch.

✅ Check: Go to Stock Summary. Are those components' quantities reduced? They should be.

Stockout Detection — Automatic Alerts

When you scan OUT and the system detects you're pulling more than what's available — meaning you've just caused a stockout — three things happen automatically:

  1. Flags the part as stockout in the dashboard (red status).
  2. Shows you a stockout report right on screen — which parts are short and by how much.
  3. Sends an email alert to you, Joe, and Max with the stockout details — automatically, in real time.
⚠️ Stockout Emails Are Triggered by You

The email only fires when a part actually goes to zero during an OUT scan. This isn't a daily report — it's an event-driven alert tied to your action. If you skip the scan and just grab the part, nobody finds out until it's too late. Scan it.

Incoming Stock Intake

"If it's not in the system, it's not in stock. Period."

Packages arrive. Great. But until someone logs them, those parts are ghosts — they exist physically but not digitally. And ghost inventory helps exactly nobody.

AI-Powered OCR

The system uses GPT-4o Vision AI to read packing lists. It's been trained specifically on QuidelOrtho packing list formats — both Container Packing Lists and Order Packing Lists. It knows the exact table layout, handles rotated/sideways documents, recognizes all our part number formats (J#####, J#####P, 1H####, 1C####, 142###, 354###), and auto-matches against all 400+ known parts in the system.

Match Status is key:

  • ✅ MATCHED — Exact match found. Will update on commit.
  • ⚠️ FUZZY — Close match (OCR misread a digit). Review and correct.
  • ❌ NOT FOUND — No match. Add to Stock Summary first, or fix the typo.

What the AI Extracts

For each packing list photo or upload, the OCR automatically pulls:

  • PO Number — Purchase Order number from the header
  • Delivery Number — Delivery # from the header
  • Tracking Number — Carrier tracking number
  • Line Items — Every part number, description, and quantity from the table

It then fuzzy-matches each extracted part number against all known parts using Levenshtein distance, substring matching, and common OCR misread corrections (I→1, O→0, S→5, B→8).

Hands-On Exercise 6.1 — OCR a Packing List
  1. Navigate to 📥 Incoming Stock in the sidebar.
  2. Click Camera or Upload to start a new batch.
  3. Take a photo of a QuidelOrtho packing list (or upload one).
  4. Watch the AI process the image — it should extract PO, tracking, and all line items.
  5. Review match status for each line. ✅ = good. ⚠️ = verify. ❌ = fix or add.
  6. Correct any fuzzy matches by clicking the part number.
  7. Click Commit to add to inventory.

✅ Verify: Check Stock Summary — quantities should have increased for committed parts.

💡 OCR Isn't Perfect — And That's OK

The AI is trained on QuidelOrtho formats and handles rotated documents, but sometimes a "1" becomes an "I" or a "J" gets dropped. The system auto-corrects common mistakes, but always review match status before committing. A 30-second review beats a 30-minute investigation later.

Ownership Moment

A shipment sitting on the dock unlogged is the same as a shipment that never arrived. The system can't count what it doesn't know about. Log it. Every time. Same day.

Key Features

WIP Columns — Counting Work-In-Progress

When analyzers are being rebuilt on the floor, parts sitting on the bench aren't in stock — they're WIP. The cycle count grid now has dynamic WIP columns for each analyzer serial number.

  • Add WIP Serial — Click "Add WIP Serial" and enter the analyzer serial number (e.g., ABC-1234). A new column appears in the grid.
  • Per-Part WIP Qty — For each part, enter how many are at that analyzer's bench. The system tracks WIP per serial number.
  • Remove WIP Serial — Click the ✕ next to a serial number to remove it. Confirmation required — "Are you sure?"
  • Grid scales dynamically — Each WIP column adds 80px to the row width. Base columns + N × 80px for WIP serials.
  • Saved & Resumed — WIP entries persist when you Save & Exit and resume later. Stored as wipEntries: [{sn, qty}].

Incoming Column

A separate Incoming column lets you record parts that are on order or in transit. This gives the full picture: Counted + WIP + Incoming = Total Expected.

Excel Export & Schedule Editing

  • Export to Excel — Any completed count can be exported as an .xlsx file with all columns (Part #, Description, System Qty, Counted, WIP, Incoming, Variance).

Smart Part Matching

The AI doesn't just read text — it matches what it reads against your actual inventory. It uses a 6-step matching cascade:

  1. Exact match — The OCR text matches a known part number perfectly
  2. Stripped match — Remove dashes, spaces, special chars and compare
  3. Prefix match — Try common QuidelOrtho prefixes (J, 1H, 0)
  4. OCR fix — Correct common misreads: I→1, O→0, S→5, B→8
  5. Substring match — If 4+ characters are contained in a known part
  6. Fuzzy match — Levenshtein distance ≤ 2 for close matches

Each line shows a confidence badge: MATCHED (green) or UNMATCHED (red). Unmatched items have an inline edit button — click the pencil icon, type the correct part number, and hit Enter.

When Things Go Sideways

"Smooth seas don't make skilled sailors. Let's simulate some storms."

Things will go wrong. Not because the system is broken — usually because reality is messy. Here's what you'll actually encounter, and exactly how to handle it.

🚨 Scenario 1

"I scanned a part but the system says NOT FOUND"

The barcode doesn't match any part in the system.

Why: Part was never added to Stock Summary, or the barcode encodes a manufacturer code that differs from our internal number.

✅ Fix: Type the part number manually. If it still doesn't match, the part needs to be added to Stock Summary first. Don't skip the scan — that creates a ghost pull. Ask Joe if you're stuck.
🚨 Scenario 2

"The camera won't scan the barcode"

Camera opens but can't read the label.

Why: Browser permissions, low light, damaged label, or non-standard barcode format.

✅ Fix: (1) Allow camera permissions in browser. (2) Better lighting — barcodes need contrast. (3) If the label is damaged, type the part number manually. Don't waste 5 minutes fighting a scanner when you can type it in 5 seconds.
🚨 Scenario 3

"I committed a batch with the wrong mode"

Meant to scan IN (returning parts) but left it on OUT. Stock went down instead of up.

Why: Mode defaults to OUT. Easy to forget to switch.

✅ Fix: Use ADJUST mode to correct the quantity to what it should be. Or make a new IN scan for double the quantity to offset. Tell Joe what happened. Don't pretend it didn't happen.
🚨 Scenario 4

"System says 10 but I'm looking at 3 on the shelf"

Digital count doesn't match physical count.

Why: Unscanned pulls. Someone took parts without scanning them out.

✅ Fix: (1) Transaction Search — filter by that part. Check the math. (2) If there's a gap, use ADJUST to set the correct quantity. (3) Tell Joe and the team. If parts are walking off without scans, we need to know.
🚨 Scenario 5

"The OCR got everything wrong on my packing slip"

Uploaded a photo and the AI misread part numbers and quantities.

Why: Faded print, handwriting, glare, unusual fonts. The AI is trained on QuidelOrtho formats and auto-corrects common misreads, but can't fix everything.

✅ Fix: Review every line before committing. ⚠️ FUZZY = close match, verify it. ❌ NOT FOUND = wrong number, fix manually. Never commit a batch you haven't reviewed line by line.
🚨 Scenario 6

"My cycle count numbers don't match"

Physically counted 8 but system expected 12. Variance = −4.

Why: Unscanned pulls, misplaced parts, or receiving errors. Variance is information, not failure.

✅ Fix: (1) Recount to be sure. (2) Check adjacent bins for misplaced stock. (3) Review Transaction Search. (4) Enter the honest count. Do not fudge numbers to avoid variance. Variance data is how we fix systemic issues.
🚨 Scenario 7

"I accidentally deleted a part from Stock Summary"

Hit trash, confirmed "Are you sure?", and the part is gone.

✅ Fix: Contact Joe immediately. The part will need to be recreated manually. Transaction history for that part is gone. This is why the system asks "Are you sure?" — it means it.
🚨 Scenario 8

"The dashboard seems frozen or shows stale data"

Numbers aren't updating after a scan.

✅ Fix: (1) Hard refresh: Ctrl+Shift+R (Win) or Cmd+Shift+R (Mac). (2) Close and reopen the tab. (3) Check WiFi. (4) Try incognito mode. If it persists, screenshot it and text Joe.
The Golden Rule of Errors
Don't hide it. Don't ignore it. Don't "fix it quietly." Flag it, communicate it, fix it together. Errors caught early are minor inconveniences. Errors caught late are investigations. Over-communicate.
