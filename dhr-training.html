What Changed

"No more guessing what goes in the box. The checklist knows."

The DHR Scanner has been completely rebuilt. The old version used AI/OCR to read documents — point your camera, hope the AI gets it right, fix what it missed. That workflow is gone.

The new DHR Scanner is a pre-loaded checklist system. Every part that belongs in a VITROS 5600 rebuild is already in the system — organized by section, with expected quantities, categories, and real-time stock levels. You don't scan documents. You fill a checklist.

"The checklist already knows what's needed. You confirm what you pulled."
Every part. Every quantity. Every stock update. In real time.

Step 1 — Create a Session

Click + New Session in the top-right corner. You'll see three fields:

FieldRequired?What to Enter
Instrument S/N✅ YesThe serial number of the analyzer being rebuilt (e.g., 08001234)

Click Create Session. The system loads the full parts checklist for that analyzer model. For the VITROS 5600, that's 22 sections and 144 parts.

Step 2 — Fill the Checklist

The session opens with every section expanded. Each section shows its parts in a table with these columns:

ColumnWhat It Shows
PARTPart number and description. Cross-reference notes appear in green italic below.
CATEGORYREQ Required · OPT Optional · TOOL Tool
BOMBill of Materials quantity — how many the spec calls for. Click to quick-fill.
QTYYour entered quantity. Click the field, type the number, press Enter or click away.
QOHQuantity On Hand — live from stock. Red = zero stock.
STATUSShows ✅ when a quantity has been entered.

Step 3 — Track Progress

The Overall Progress bar at the top shows two numbers:

  • X/62 required — How many required parts you've filled vs. total required
  • X/144 total — How many parts (of all types) you've filled vs. total

Each section header also shows its own progress: X/Y required · X/Z total with a mini progress bar.

Step 4 — Export (Optional)

Click Export at any time to download the session as an Excel file. The export includes every part, section, BOM qty, scanned qty, QOH, and status.

Step 5 — Complete Session

When the build is done, click the green ✅ Complete button. This locks the session — no more edits. The session moves to a "completed" state and is visible in the session history.

⚠️ Complete Is Final

Once you click Complete, the session is locked. All quantity fields become read-only. Make sure you've verified everything before completing. If you need to make a correction after completing, contact Joe.

Part Categories

Every part is tagged with one of three categories:

REQ Required — 62 parts

Must be installed for the build to pass QA. These parts are tracked in the required progress counter. If you skip a required part, the progress bar will show it.

OPT Optional — 77 parts

Installed as-needed based on the specific analyzer's condition. Not counted toward "required" progress, but still tracked in the total count.

TOOL Tools — 5 parts

Reusable tools needed during the build (torque wrenches, alignment tools, etc.). Tool entries do not deduct from stock — entering a qty for a tool records it in the session but doesn't change QOH or create audit/SAP entries.

Cross-Reference Notes

Some parts appear in one section but are physically installed during another section's work. These show green italic notes below the description — for example:

  • "Parts installed in section 6.2..."
  • "J30135 Assembly Waste Aux Bottle..."
  • "Will be installed as..."

These notes are informational — they help you understand where and how the part fits in the overall build.

Collapsible Sections

Each section can be collapsed or expanded by clicking the section header. Collapse sections you've finished to reduce visual clutter. The progress indicator on each header stays visible even when collapsed.

💡 Service Activities (No Parts)

Sections 5.1 (MOD Status Verification) and 5.23 (Label) are service activities with no parts. They show the message "Service activity — verify and proceed" instead of a parts table. These exist as checkpoints in the workflow.

Entering Quantities

"Type the number. Stock updates instantly. No extra steps."

Two Ways to Enter

1. Click the QTY Field

Click the — in the QTY column. A number input appears. Type your quantity and press Enter or click outside the field. The quantity saves immediately.

2. Quick-Fill from BOM

Click the BOM number (the bold number in the BOM column). The QTY field instantly fills with the BOM quantity. One click — done. Use this when you're pulling exactly what the spec calls for.

What Happens When You Enter a Qty

The moment you enter or change a quantity, three things happen simultaneously:

⚠️ Stock Updates Are Immediate

There is no "commit" button for individual entries. The moment you enter a number and move to the next field, stock is deducted. This is by design — it keeps inventory accurate in real-time. If you make a mistake, see the Corrections section below.

Delta Corrections

Made a mistake? Just change the number. The system calculates the delta (difference) and adjusts accordingly:

ScenarioWhat You DoWhat Happens to Stock
Entered 2, should be 3Change QTY from 2 → 31 more OUT from stock (delta = +1)
Entered 3, should be 1Change QTY from 3 → 12 returned IN to stock (delta = −2)
Entered 2, should be 0Clear the QTY field2 returned IN to stock (delta = −2)
Entered 2, should be 2No change neededNothing — delta is zero

Every correction creates its own audit log and SAP staging entry. The audit trail shows exactly what happened: 📤 J09974: 1 OUT → QOH 14 or 📥 J09974: 2 IN → QOH 17.

✅ Corrections Are Safe

You don't need to undo or delete anything. Just change the number to what it should be. The system handles the math. This is the same delta logic used in the Scan Kiosk ADJUST mode — it works both directions.

Tools — No Stock Impact

Parts tagged as TOOL are handled differently. When you enter a quantity for a tool:

  • The quantity is recorded in the session ✅
  • No stock deduction occurs ❌
  • No audit log entry is created ❌
  • No SAP staging record is created ❌

Tools are reusable — they get used during the build but go back on the shelf. The checklist tracks that they were present, not consumed.

Hands-On Exercise 4.1 — Enter and Correct Quantities
  1. Open a DHR session (or create one with a test serial number).
  2. Find section 5.10 Signal Reagent Metering.
  3. Click the BOM number for 1H4534 (PROBE, SIGNAL REAGENT) — it should show 2. Watch the QTY fill to 2 instantly.
  4. Check the QOH column — did it decrease by 2?
  5. Now change the QTY from 2 → 1. Check QOH — it should increase by 1 (delta correction).
  6. Clear the QTY field entirely. QOH should return to its original value.
  7. Note: Use the training dashboard for this exercise — not production.

Stock Integration

"The DHR doesn't just track what you pulled — it moves the numbers."

The DHR Scanner is directly connected to your live inventory. Every quantity you enter flows through the same pipeline as the Scan Kiosk. This means:

  • Stock Summary reflects DHR pulls immediately — no delay, no batch processing
  • Transaction Search shows every DHR entry with the analyzer serial number
  • SAP Push includes DHR entries — they stage with movement type 261 (OUT) or 101 (IN for corrections)
  • Cycle Count will show accurate numbers because DHR deductions happened in real-time

QOH Column — Your Live Stock Indicator

The QOH column in the checklist shows the current quantity on hand for each part, pulled live from the stock table. Key visual cues:

QOH DisplayMeaning
Normal number (white)Part is in stock. Good to go.
0 (red)Zero stock. You're pulling from an empty bin.
— (dash)Part not found in stock table. May be a tool or a new part not yet added.
⚠️ Pulling from Zero Stock

The system allows you to enter a quantity even when QOH is zero — it won't block you. But the stock will go negative, which flags an issue. If QOH shows 0, physically verify the part is available before entering a quantity. If you pull a part that isn't in the system, the stock deduction is skipped (no crash, no error — it just records the session entry without affecting stock).

DHR entries tie into Analyzer Analysis because every OUT is tagged with the instrument serial number from the session. This means when you look up an analyzer in Analyzer Analysis, you'll see all the parts that were consumed during its rebuild — both from the Scan Kiosk and from the DHR Scanner.

💡 Two Systems, One Inventory

The Scan Kiosk and DHR Scanner both update the same stock table. Don't double-count. If you already scanned a part OUT through the Kiosk during a build, don't enter it again in the DHR. One source of truth. Pick the tool that fits the moment — Kiosk for individual scans, DHR for the full build checklist.

Session List

The main DHR Scanner page shows all sessions — both active and completed. Each session card displays:

  • Instrument S/N and model
  • Status — In Progress or Completed
  • Started date
  • Progress — parts filled vs. total

Click any session to expand it and see the full checklist. Click again to collapse.

Multiple Sessions

You can have multiple sessions active at once — one for each analyzer being rebuilt. Each session tracks its own instrument serial number and its own set of part quantities. Stock deductions are shared across all sessions (because it's the same inventory).

💡 One Session Per S/N

Create one session per analyzer serial number. If you need to rebuild the same analyzer again (rework), complete the first session and create a new one. This preserves the history — you can always go back and see what was pulled the first time.

When Things Go Sideways

"Most DHR issues are quantity mistakes — and they're all fixable."

🚨 Scenario 1

"I entered the wrong quantity"

Typed 5 instead of 2. Stock went down by 5 instead of 2.

Why: Fat fingers happen.

✅ Fix: Change the QTY to the correct number (2). The system calculates the delta (−3) and returns 3 units to stock automatically. Check the QOH column — it should reflect the correction. No need to undo anything.
🚨 Scenario 2

"I already scanned this part through the Kiosk"

Part was scanned OUT via Scan Kiosk earlier. Now the DHR checklist shows it again.

Why: The DHR checklist is pre-populated with all expected parts regardless of prior scans.

✅ Fix: Don't enter it again in the DHR — that would double-deduct from stock. Leave the QTY as — (blank) for parts already handled through the Kiosk. Or enter the qty and immediately use the Kiosk IN mode to add it back. Best practice: choose one method per build — Kiosk or DHR, not both for the same part.
🚨 Scenario 3

"QOH shows 0 but I have the part in my hand"

The shelf has parts but the system says zero.

Why: Previous transactions weren't logged, or incoming stock wasn't received into the system.

✅ Fix: (1) Check Transaction Search for recent activity on that part. (2) Check Incoming Stock — was a delivery received but not committed? (3) If the physical count is right, use Stock Summary to ADJUST the qty first. (4) Then enter the DHR quantity. Tell Joe.
🚨 Scenario 4

"A part I need isn't on the checklist"

You're installing a part that doesn't appear in any section.

Why: The checklist covers the standard 144-part BOM. Non-standard parts, field modifications, or special replacements may not be listed.

✅ Fix: Scan the part through the Scan Kiosk using OUT mode with the analyzer's serial number. This ensures it's tracked in Transaction Search and Analyzer Analysis even though it's not on the DHR checklist. Tell Joe — the part may need to be added to the BOM for future builds.
🚨 Scenario 5

"I completed the session by accident"

Clicked the green Complete button too early. Now everything is locked.

✅ Fix: Contact Joe immediately. A completed session can only be reopened with admin intervention. The stock changes from the session are already recorded — they don't need to be reversed unless quantities were wrong. If corrections are needed, use the Scan Kiosk ADJUST mode for individual parts.
🚨 Scenario 6

"The session won't load or shows an error"

Clicked on a session but the checklist is blank or shows a loading error.

✅ Fix: (1) Hard refresh: Ctrl+Shift+R (Win) or Cmd+Shift+R (Mac). (2) Check WiFi — the system needs a live connection to Supabase. (3) Try a different browser or incognito mode. (4) If it persists, screenshot the error and text Joe.
